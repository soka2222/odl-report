Background And Artifact

Referring to the subsystems established based on the attained BIM model and the BIM Execution Plan (BEP) delivered in concept 1, the stakeholders involved in the BIM model development phase comprise of a wide range of parties whose contributions are essential for producing, coordinating, and validating the project information. These include the Employer, the Supervision Consultant (BIM Manager and BIM Coordinators), and the Contractor represented by Project Managers, BIM Coordinators, BIM Modellers, BIM Document Controllers, and discipline-specific designers (architecture and structure).

Furthermore, to support modelling and the activities these stakeholders can carry out, a range of datasets is required. These datasets typically include the design project information which contains design drawing with measurement, volume, materials and documented approval. Then, 3d discipline-specific BIM modelling files, in this case, for architectural and structural elements, can be built. The complete overview of stakeholders, responsibilities, interoperability, datasets involved and their potential issue is summarized and explained in the Appendix II.2 .

This project provides two principal subsystems, Architectural and Structural, which can be integrated into a unified System Breakdown Structure (SBS), as shown in Appendix II.1 . The SBS is arranged according to the project work classifications to create a clear and logical hierarchy of systems in all building components.

The semantic information of every element and sub-element taken from the previous model was updated to ensure consistency in names, categories, and functional descriptions. Moreover, we also added the custom-modelled components (as shown in figure 2.1) into the model, so the full set of deliverables for this assignment is complete.

To support clear communication among all stakeholders, every sub-element, element, and subsystem is assigned a unique code or ID.

Reflection

. Challenges in Working with BIM Models

The team experienced interoperability challenges related to combining architectural and structural models. Although both models were generated in Revit, there are differences found in modelling standard. Some elements did not follow a consistent classification which difficult to assign into SBS structure. As a result, we need to do extra effort to standardize the parameters. This demonstrates the distinction between technical operability, semantic interoperability, and organizational interoperability. In this case, the issue is semantic interoperability.

Finally, the model size and level of detail also affected working time. More complex the components in the model, will increase the time needed to assign the SBS, especially when the adjustments are needed to see in the multiple view. In practice, it is important to maintain to remove irrelevant elements for the PoI and applying clear LOD rule to help the SBS development.

Improved Decision-Making Through BIM Model and SBS

The BIM model helps to decide the Level of Development (LOD) that appropriately be used in multi discipline models in this case Architecture and Structure. Establish the correct LOD ensures the level of information in the model align with the needs of coordination and decision making at each project stage.

By organising the project into well-defined system (architectural, structural etc.) and assigning SBS IDs to each BIM element, the team gain a clear and consistent structured information for analysing the model, allowing them to understand how one system influences another, identify which interface points carry the highest risk of clashes, and recognise areas where design may require adjustment. This enables the decision related to placement, routing, or modification of elements can be made earlier and with greater confidence, reducing the likelihood of conflicts emerging later in the process and supporting smoother coordination across disciplines.

Potential Future Enhancements and Integration Opportunities

With more time or resources, we would like to integrate the other system such as MEP to increase the value of the model for coordination because most interface risks in WWTP facilities occur at structure with MEP. To support this, the SBS structure must be extended to incorporate MEP components using standardized naming and parameters.

In addition. We would improve the current structural and architectural integration by formalizing standards in both models and defining clear rules for Level of Detail (LOD). This improvement would improve the workability of the models.
