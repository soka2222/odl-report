Concept 7: Information Systems for Future Construction

Open Design Learning (ODL) ‚Äî Information System for the Construction Industry

BACKGROUND AND ARTIFACT

Supporting future-oriented building operation and performance tracking, this assignment includes the setup of a basic digital twin structure in Autodesk Tandem, which considers temperature and humidity sensors as IoT-related assets. These sensors were classified within the Tandem environment and linked to dedicated parameters for Temperature and Humidity, making it possible to represent indoor environmental conditions directly in the asset dashboards. Through this process, the digital twin was able not only to store the building physical component‚Äôs information but also prepare the model for future data-driven monitoring. This way of working aligns with the idea of instead seeing BIM model as a design and coordination tools alone, it also works as long-term information system, helping stakeholders to evaluate comfort conditions, detect changes in environmental performance, and give a better understanding of how the building behaves over time

REFLECTION

  • Challenges

One of the biggest challenges in developing this artifact was to align the temperature and humidity sensors properly into structured data so the Tandem could translate them into meaningful assets. The temperature and humidity parameter could be added, however it demands ancillary workflow to develop a complete dashboard. This process requires additional manual work and verification for ensuring correct asset classification, clear naming conventions, and consistent parameter assignment. Furthermore, it is apparent that the dashboard results provide the present and complete weather parameters, while for the real usage of the system, it is necessary to include real operational data. This assignment scope did not include live sensor streaming. This meant that the sensors remained part of a model-based monitoring setup, but without the ability to validate the full connection between real physical devices and the digital twin environment. As a result, the artifact represents a prepared and structured foundation for future monitoring, but not yet a fully functioning real-time performance system.

  • Future Improvement and Opportunities

If more time and resources were available, the digital twin could be expanded by connecting the temperature and humidity sensors to real-time data streams, so that environmental values could be updated continuously within Tandem dashboards. This would allow stakeholders to observe trends over time, identify abnormal patterns such as unexpected humidity increases or unstable temperatures, and respond faster to operational risks. In addition, integrating the sensor data with other facility management systems would create stronger decision-making support, since the model could then be used for automated warnings, performance reporting, and preventive maintenance actions. By combining a well-structured BIM-based asset model with real sensor feedback, the project could move closer to a true lifecycle digital twin system where the building is managed through continuous information updates, rather than only through static documentation after handover.
