Management Summary

The Project of Wastewater Treatment Plan, as a part of Jakarta Sewerage Development Plan Zone 1, was used as a project of interest in this assignment. The project concerns the construction of a Wastewater Treatment Plant (WWTP) as part of the Jakarta Sewerage System in Zone-1. This development aims to improve water quality and sanitation access within several key urban areas of DKI Jakarta. The project is part of a broader governmental sanitation program that covers the central metropolitan region.

The WWTP is located in Pluit, North Jakarta, on a site of approximately 3.9 hectares and is designed to treat up to 240,000 m¬≥ of wastewater per day. Once completed, the system will serve parts of Central Jakarta, West Jakarta, and North Jakarta, covering a combined population of roughly 989,000 residents across eight districts. As the overall project is too large, this PoI only focuses on one specific structure, named Sludge Treatment Building, as marked in the image below.

This PoI provides architectural and structural design information that enables the integration of multiple disciplines within a Building Information Modelling (BIM) environment. This complexity level generates suitable exploration for BIM application and supports the information system across different stages of the project life cycle.

An improved BIM model was developed using Autodesk Revit to integrate architectural and structural elements. This model serves as a basis for further BIM-enabled analysis and information system application. By utilizing Navisworks, clash of detection can be performed to identify and resolve spatial conflicts between disciplines. To enable a 4D BIM simulation, the project schedule was integrated in Synchro Pro, allowing the construction sequence to be visualized and analysed. Later this 4D model also utilized to enable stakeholders identify safety hazards and mitigate it at the early phase.

For asset and maintenance management, Autodesk Tandem was applied to structure asset-related information and demonstrate how BIM data can support operational use after the construction phase. Autodesk Insight was utilized to illustrate how BIM data can be used to support environmental performance evaluation.

Finally, newer technologies could also enhance decision making beyond construction such as digital twins, VR machines, and automated sensors. With more time and resources, the PoI could be further expanded by integrating real time IoT data sources to support better decision in a whole life cycle.
