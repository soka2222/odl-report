Concept 6: Information Systems for Safety

Open Design Learning (ODL) ‚Äî Information System for the Construction Industry

BACKGROUND AND ARTIFACT

This PoI‚Äôs safety management primarily concerns the construction phase, where the multiple stakeholders interact dynamically on site. The following table explains stakeholders who are involved in ensuring safety, what datasets are integrated and its information systems source.

StakeholderRole in Safety Decision-MakingKey DatasetsInformation System / Tool
ClientSets safety objectives and requirements; reviews safety risks at a strategic level and approves construction approachSafety visualization outputs, high-level risk scenariosBIM visualizations (Navisworks), 4D simulations
Project ManagerUses safety analysis outputs to plan construction sequencing, reduce risks, and make informed safety decisionsConstruction sequence scenarios, identified safety risksSynchro Pro (4D BIM), Navisworks
Contractor / Site EngineerPlans and executes construction activities safely based on sequencing and spatial constraintsConstruction schedule, temporary structures, equipment layoutSynchro Pro, Navisworks

Safety Officer (HSE)Identifies hazards and evaluates risk zones using spatial and temporal informationHazard locations, unsafe access areas, fall-risk zonesNavisworks (walkthrough), Synchro Pro

The artifact developed for this concept is a BIM-based safety walkthrough, integrating the coordinated BIM model with the construction schedule. The chosen timeline is right after the structural components have been fully constructed and before the stairs and architectural components have been installed, such as the wall. This creates the construction area with a high risk of fall.Subsequently, the outer area of buildings has been installed with temporary scaffolding, which avoids falling risk. However, the inner area has not been fully noticed, for instance the stairs area. Since the stair area has not been constructed, it resembles a void with high falling risk and requires temporary safety structures such as temporary guardrails to prevent fall hazards before permanent elements are constructed. REFLECTION

  • Challenges

While generating the required artifacts to address this concept, we encountered several of challenges. In the original model, temporary structure and construction equipment are not included, meanwhile many critical hazards are emerged by temporary conditions. Therefore, the actual site condition must be assumed by examining the schedule and discussing with the project team. Then temporary elements, such as heavy equipment and scaffolding, must be manually added to the model.

Since the construction phase is dynamic, we must examine each stage of the construction phase using 4D modelling to identify the potential unsafe conditions of the building. However, the 4D model is developed in Synchro Pro which does not specifically provide the virtual walkthrough function that available in Navisworks. Consequently, we must simultaneously use two different software applications to accurately identify, assess and resolve safety-related issues.

  • How the information supports decision making

Using visual and spatial representations helps project managers to improve hazard recognition compared to traditional safety plans,because many safety risks are emergent and spatial in nature (e.g. fall risks). Using 4D views to identify hazards early and decide controls before work starts is known as ‚Äúprevention through planning‚Äù This also supports communication during toolbox meetings and safety briefings, because the risks can be easily informed to the staff and subcontractors. This aligns with Borrman chapter 21, BIM enables proactive safety management by visualizing construction phases before execution.

  • Future Improvement and Opportunities

If there were additional time and resources, the BIM-based safety walkthrough could be enhanced by integrating it into an immersive Virtual Reality (VR) safety walk, as demonstrated in the guest lecture by Stephan Klotz. Linking the existing 4D BIM model to a VR environment, stakeholders would be able to experience construction hazards right in the location at the certain time and decide the mitigations before work starts. The workflow will use Synchro as source, and then the model will be published using iTwin / Cesium. Afterwads, it will be rendered using Unreal Engine for enabling first-person walkthrough. Finally, it will be imported into Cavrnus to allow multiple stakeholders to join the same session at the sam time to discuss about the safety hazard. This experience would significantly improve risk perception and understanding, specifically for non-technical stakeholders such as clients or investors.
