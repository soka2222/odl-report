Section A: BIM Project Execution Plan Overview

To ensure the effective implementation of Building Information Modeling (BIM) within this project, this BIM Project Execution Plan (BEP) establishes the framework, processes, and responsibilities required for structured digital collaboration throughout all project phases. The BEP defines the intended BIM uses such as design authoring, model coordination, information management, and construction planning and describes the procedures for executing these uses consistently across the project lifecycle.

The BEP outlines the agreements between all involved project parties regarding modeling standards, data exchange protocols, workflow coordination, and the digital platforms that will be utilized. It specifies the roles and responsibilities of contributors, the required level of detail for BIM models, and guidelines for sharing and reviewing project information. By defining these aspects clearly, BIM can be implemented efficiently, transparently, and with reduced risk of miscommunication.

BIM is applied on this project to support digital engineering principles and to enhance the accuracy, coordination, and traceability of project information. The BEP serves as the central document guiding the development, review, approval, and management of all BIM deliverables, ensuring that stakeholders consistently work with up-to-date and validated information.

Moreover, this BIM Execution Plan also establishes a framework for applying BIM as an integrated information system for sustainability analysis, construction safety assessment, asset and maintenance management and future digital improvements through the project lifecycle. Where necessary, additional documentation such as workflow diagrams, naming conventions, and information exchange requirements may be appended to this BEP to provide further detail and operational clarity.

Project Overview

The project concerns the construction of a Wastewater Treatment Plant (WWTP) as part of the Jakarta Sewerage System in Zone-1. This development aims to improve water quality and sanitation access within several key urban areas of DKI Jakarta. The project is part of a broader governmental sanitation program that covers the central metropolitan region.

The WWTP is located in Pluit, North Jakarta, on a site of approximately 3.9 hectares and is designed to treat up to 240,000 m¬≥ of wastewater per day. Once completed, the system will serve parts of Central Jakarta, West Jakarta, and North Jakarta, covering a combined population of roughly 989,000 residents across eight districts.

Key technical characteristics of the project include:

  • A planned service population of approximately 989,389 people within Zone-1.
  • The WWTP utilizes A2O + Membrane Bio-Reactor (MBR) technology, supported by high-rate filtration processes.
  • Sewer installation makes use of pipe jacking, while pump station structures are constructed using pneumatic caisson methods.

This overview provides the foundational context for BIM implementation throughout the project, ensuring that the digital strategy aligns with the scale, complexity, and technical requirements of the wastewater infrastructure.

Section K: Delivery Strategy / Contract

  • Delivery and Contracting Strategy for the project:

To successfully implement BIM under the selected contract type (Unit Price & Lump Sum), the project requires:

  • Clear BIM roles and responsibilities defined in the BIM Execution Plan.
  • Mandatory adherence to the BIM standards, modeling requirements, and file exchange protocols.
  • A unified Common Data Environment (ACC) for document and model control.
  • Regular BIM coordination meetings and milestone-based model submissions.
  • BIM deliverables aligned with contract milestones (Design Development, Construction Documents, Construction, Closeout).
  • These measures ensure that BIM processes support accurate design, efficient coordination, and reliable construction information for the Sludge Treatment Building.
  • Team Selection Procedure:

Future team members involved in BIM activities will be selected based on:

  • Demonstrated experience with BIM tools (Revit, Navisworks, Synchro).
  • Ability to comply with the project‚Äôs BIM standards and modeling requirements.
  • Proven capability to deliver LOD 350 models for their discipline.
  • Coordination experience in multidisciplinary industrial/process facilities.
  • Only qualified individuals and subcontractors meeting these criteria will participate in model development and coordination workflows.
  • BIM Contracting Procedure:

BIM requirements should be explicitly written into future contracts as follows:

  • The BIM Execution Plan (BEP) becomes a contractual document binding for all parties.
  • All subcontractors must submit discipline models following the LOD, naming, and data requirements specified in the BEP.
  • Model submissions, clash detection obligations, and coordination meeting attendance are required contractual activities.
  • Deliverables such as federated models, clash reports, shop drawings, and record models must be provided in specified formats.
  • Any deviation from BIM standards may result in rejection of deliverables or the need for rework at the contractor‚Äôs expense.
